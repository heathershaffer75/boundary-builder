# 🧱 Boundary Builder

A practical, trauma-informed **Primary Mental Health (PMH)** group activity designed to help patients understand, develop, and communicate **healthy boundaries**.

This tool focuses on turning insight into action by answering:

> **“What do I actually say, and how do I say it?”**

---

## 🧠 Purpose

This app supports:

* Understanding different types of boundaries
* Recognizing unhealthy patterns
* Building self-respect and clarity
* Improving communication skills
* Reducing resentment and burnout

---

## 🎯 Core Concept

Boundaries are not about controlling others.

> **Boundaries are about clearly communicating what works and what doesn’t for you.**

They help define:

* what you will accept
* what you will not accept
* how you respond

---

## 🧩 Sections Included

### 🚫 No Boundaries

* Needs ignored or unclear
* Frequent overextending
* Builds resentment over time

---

### 🫠 Weak Boundaries

* Inconsistent or unclear limits
* Saying “no” but giving in
* Driven by guilt or pressure

---

### 🧊 Rigid Boundaries

* Emotional walls
* Avoidance of closeness
* Protection through disconnection

---

### 🧱 Healthy Boundaries

* Clear, direct communication
* Respect for self and others
* Consistent and flexible

---

### 🗣️ Boundary Practice

* Real-life language examples
* How to say “no”
* How to communicate limits clearly

---

### 🧠 Boundary Myths

* “Boundaries are selfish”
* “People will be mad at me”
* “I should be able to handle everything”

---

## 🔍 Each Section Includes

* **Plain-language explanation**
* **What it looks like in real life**
* **Common problems and patterns**
* **Example boundary statements**
* **Discussion questions for group use**

---

## 🧑‍⚕️ How to Use in Group

### Option 1: Self-Reflection

* Ask: “Which type do you relate to most?”
* Explore patterns and impact

---

### Option 2: Skill Practice

* Use the “Boundary Practice” section
* Have patients:

  * rewrite statements
  * practice saying them out loud

---

### Option 3: Scenario Discussion

* Ask:

  * “What would you say here?”
  * “What makes this hard?”
  * “What would a healthy boundary sound like?”

---
